             Projectile Weapon Damages in OTHER SUNS
               (Preliminary draft for 2nd Edition)
                 COPYRIGHT 1990 by N. C. Shapero
                       (Message 1 of 2)

All rifle and pistol ammunition is caseless.  Damages assume  the 
standard  propellant charges (crazies who want to hot-load  their 
own ammunition should discuss modifications to the damages listed 
with their referee).

                    Ammunition Caliber Codes:

               A     5.5 mm             G    11.0 mm
               B     6.5 mm             H    11.5 mm
               C     8.0 mm             I    12.0 mm
               D     9.0 mm             J    15.0 mm
               E     9.5 mm             K    17.5 mm
               F    10.0 mm             L    20.0 mm

                        Ammunition Types

     1    Standard solid round

     2    Mushrooming  "hollow point"

          Poor on armor penetration.  After penetration, pain and 
          injury  is  more  severe  than  with  standard  rounds.  
          Double  all  damages after penetration  (both  END  and 
          HIT).   Taxes  on this type of ammunition result  in  a 
          retail price twice that of standard ammunition for  the 
          same  caliber.  The expected roll for a  given  caliber 
          Type  2 round is roughly 70% of the roll for  the  same 
          caliber  Type 1 round.  There is also a 5%  penalty  to 
          the "to-hit" value for Type 2 ammunition.

     3    Special surface coated armor piercer

          Specially coated with a near-frictionless material  and 
          with  a hotter load, this armor piercing ammunition  is 
          less  effective at causing body trauma than a  standard 
          round.   Halve  all  damage (both END  and  HIT)  after 
          penetration.   Taxes on this type of ammunition  result 
          in a retail price three times that of standard  ammuni-
          tion  for  the same caliber.  The expected roll  for  a 
          given caliber Type 3 round is roughly 140% of the  roll 
          for the same caliber Type 1 round.  There is also a 10% 
          penalty to the "to-hit" value for Type 3 ammunition.

     4    Match ammunition

          Match  ammunition  is designed for  accuracy  at  range 
          without consideration for penetration or damage  poten-
          tial.  Match ammunition costs only 75% of what standard 
          Type  1 ammunition costs (taxes, which are by  far  the 
          largest component in the retail cost of ammunition, are 
          kept  intentionally low by the OverGovernment).   There 
          is a 25% bonus to the "to-hit" value for Type 4 ammuni-
          tion
