Brief Background

The Mahaoli are a bipedal tailed aquatic mammalian species native 
to  Istrana, a terrestrial world with a surface  acceleration  of 
0.98 gees.  Istrana orbits Sisanek, a G1 main sequence star, at a 
distance of 1.03 astronomical units.  The terrestrial mammal most

closely resembling the Mahaoli is Lutra canadensis, the  American 
otter; it is, however, only a vague resemblance.

The  basic  unit of Mahaoli society is the "cluster":  one  adult 
alpha  male,  one adult alpha female, four adult male  betas  and 
four  adult  female betas, together with offspring  and  "helper" 
adults (non-breeders).  A typical cluster will number some thirty 
five  individuals,  ten of whom are breeding adults,  twenty  are 
adolescents  or  children,  and five  are  non-breeding  "helper" 
individuals.   The alphas are generally, though not  always,  the 
eldest  members of the cluster; half the betas of a  cluster  are 
typically adult offspring of the alpha pair of that cluster.

Mahaoli are considerably more gregarious than Humans.

The  Mahaoli were first contacted by the H'Reli during the  Third 
Expansion;  their  homeworld lies within what  is  today  Combine 
space.   The Mahaoli do not have a representative on the  Central 
Committee; they are instead represented by the H'Reli, the Altani

the Confederacy, the Litaran League, and the Vispoka Assembly are

Assembly space.

Names among the Mahaoli

A Mahaoli takes part of his name from his cluster, part from  his 
position  within the cluster, and part from choice at  adulthood.  
Mahaoli  cluster names have historically been derived  from  geo-
graphic place names.  More recently, with the departure of  large 
number of Mahaoli from their homeworld, cluster names have  begun 
to  appear  that are clearly derived from  words  in  non-Mahaoli 
languages  or are based on employment  specializations.   Cluster 
names derived from occupations typically (though not exclusively) 
end in -ti or -si.  Given/adult choice names most commonly end in 
-a.

Designators of position within cluster:

 
     Val = Alpha/leader male
     Mir = Alpha/leader female
     Ko = Beta male
     Sal = Beta female
     Ran = "helper" individual

Common Cluster names (and their meanings):

     Teeka = Teeka river
     Varan = Varan mountains
     Chell = Chell islands
     Vokti = Star plotter (navigator)
     Nisenti = Starship engineer
     Ntensi = Interstellar explorer

Common Given/adult choice names (and their meanings):

     Chensa = Quick witted
     Bropa = Lazy and clever
 
     Vandropa = Seeker after many females
     Iskanda = Seeker after many males
     Tokra = Jester
     Morga = Guzzleguts
     Funara = Prankster

A full Mahaoli name is of the form <Given name><cluster  position 
designator><cluster  name>.   For example:   Funaravalvokti  (The 
alpha  male, prankster, of the star  plotter/navigator  cluster).  
In common address, only the given name is used; in slightly  more 
formal address, the given name with the cluster position designa-
tor  attached  is  used.  So Funaravalvokti  would  be  known  to 
friends and associates as Funara, and would likely be  introduced 
as  Funaraval to new acquaintences.  The full name is  only  used 
for official (i.e. government) matters or in records keeping.

Mahaoli Languages

Healaoie

The  principal  language  of  the  Mahaolan  Confederacy  worlds.  
Roughly two thirds of the Mahaoli are native to one or another of 
the  worlds  of the Confederacy and speak this  as  their  native 
language.

Mirmiroiya

The principal language of the Litaran League worlds.  Roughly ten 
percent of the Mahaoli speak Mirmiroiya as their native language.

Imaoha
 

The  principal language of the Vispoka Assembly worlds.  MOST  of 
the  remaining  Mahaoli live on worlds owing  allegiance  to  the 
Vispoka Assembly.

The Mahaolan Outlook

(1)  There is a passion for pleasure.

(2)  The three cardinal virtues are: honesty, responsibility, and 
     a sense of humor.

(3)  A sense of proportion, and an ethical sense are also consid-
     ered important.

(4)  Power over another adult intelligent being has no justifica-
     tion.  It may, under certain circumstances, be necessary  -- 
     but this does not justify the power, only explain it.

 
(5)  There  is  a  basic natural law  governing  all  intelligent 
     beings.

(6)  Under  this  law, all intelligent life forms  are  basically 
     equal.

Attitudes of the Mahaolan Middle Class:  Equality among  sophonts 
is  assumed to be axiomatic. The world view is optimistic  (hedo-
nist),  and dignity is considered to be a basic virtue.   Poverty 
is  regarded as a vice.  The "species" memory of the  Mahaoli  is 
extremely  long.   They  are capable of assuming  a  position  of 
galactic dominance at need; they have no desire to do so,  howev-
er.

Attitudes  of the Mahaolan Elite:  Power evokes respect, but  the 
greatest political force among the Mahaoli is still diplomacy and 
consensus democracy.  The hero image is the negotiator who satis-
fies ALL parties in a dispute.  Sophisticated aliens evoke  wari-
ness,  and the primitive masses are regarded as children  (to  be 
protected,  cared for, and nurtured).  Ambition is  tolerated  in 
others.  The greatest weakness of the Mahaolan elite is a lack of 
military aggressiveness.  They are always ready to "borrow"  from 
others'  cultures.  Dilettantism is strongly frowned upon.   Lei-
sure is a virtue, to be sought be all.
